Section 04
Sprint 5 Priorities
March 4 – March 18, 2026  ·  7 initiatives
1
Paid Media Transition — Meta → LinkedIn
Shift paid budget from Facebook to LinkedIn to capture IT MSP conversions where the audience actually is. The Feb CL data confirms Facebook generates only 5 Stage 2+ losses — the issue isn't qualified pipeline quality, it's reaching the right audience upstream. LinkedIn puts us in front of IT decision-makers by role and company size.
RevenuePipeline
2
Re-engagement of Closed Lost — Missing Capabilities ($100K Opportunity)
5 opps ($4.4K MRR) were lost in Feb specifically because of product gaps that have since been addressed. That's just February — the cumulative pool is a $100K opportunity. Targeted outreach campaign with personalized messaging tied to the exact capability they needed. These prospects already know us and took the meeting once.
Revenue
3
Tigerpaw Migration Campaign — April 1 Deadline
Tigerpaw customers are actively searching for a new platform ahead of April 1. This is a high-urgency, time-boxed opportunity. Campaign focused on business continuity, zero disruption migration, and the risk of waiting. Churn prevention for existing Rev.io customers who may be evaluating and new acquisition for Tigerpaw refugees.
Churn PreventionRevenue
4
Raving Fans Program + G2 Campaign Launch
Celebrate our best customers publicly and activate them as brand advocates. Simultaneously launch a G2 review campaign — peer reviews are a primary buying signal in the IT MSP market where prospects heavily research before talking to sales. Reviews build credibility that no ad spend can replicate.
BrandPipeline
5
Product Marketing Shift — Real-Time Promotion vs. Named Launches
Move from big-bang named launch cycles to continuous real-time feature promotion. Every release is a reason to be in the market. Gives sales fresh talking points weekly, keeps Rev.io top-of-mind, and reduces dependence on infrequent launch moments that can fall flat if timing is off.
BrandPipeline
6
Multi-Touch Attribution & Lead Scoring
Build the data infrastructure to understand which channels and touches are actually driving pipeline. Lead scoring feeds bot intelligence — the smarter and more structured our data, the more RevItUp and Claudio can prioritize, personalize, and action autonomously. This is the foundation layer everything else depends on.
Bot IntelPipeline
7
April Show Prep 🎪 — Bootcamp, MSP Summit, Channel Partners, Skyswitch & Kaseya
Five events converging in April. Pre-show outreach campaigns, booth strategy and materials, meeting booking targets, and post-show follow-up sequences need to be designed and launched now. Shows succeed or fail based on the prep done 3–4 weeks prior — not the week of.
