Executive Summary
IT MSP Segment Performance — 2025
Overview

In 2025, marketing successfully activated the IT MSP segment — 38% of total leads generated were IT MSPs, validating the segment's response to our go-to-market efforts. Across all products, we created $502K in pipeline and $73.9K in MRR, with the majority of that value coming from non-PSA products (gross majority being Billing).

Key Takeaways
  • ✓
    IT MSPs are responding to marketing. 38% of all 2025 leads were IT MSPs — strong top-of-funnel traction from website demand and tradeshows.
  • $
    Billing is the primary value driver. Billing-led buyers generated $328K pipeline and $56.4K MRR — ~3× more valuable per deal than PSA-only buyers.
  • !
    PSA is a conversion problem, not a demand problem. 256 PSA leads, but 9.8% close rate and $700 avg MRR — product maturity and sales motion, not marketing.
Strategic Direction
🎯 The Play: Land on Billing, Expand into PSA

The data supports a "land and expand" motion — acquire IT MSPs through Rev.io's competitive Billing offering where win rates and deal values are demonstrably stronger, then run a structured cross-sell into PSA as the product continues to develop and differentiate.

With $1.99M allocated to IT MSP in 2026 (up from $784K), we have the budget to scale what's working — Billing-led demand generation — while building the cross-sell infrastructure that converts Billing customers into PSA adopters over time.

Strategic Playbook
What the data tells us to do next
1
Scale Billing-Led Demand Gen
Billing buyers deliver 3× the MRR of PSA-only buyers ($960 vs $700 avg MRR). Double down on channels and messaging that attract Billing-interested IT MSPs. Website/demo request is the #1 driver (48% of pipeline) — invest in CRO and GEO strategy to widen that funnel.
2
Build the PSA Cross-Sell Motion
11 of 25 PSA wins already came from Billing customers. Formalize this — create a dedicated cross-sell track with sales plays, nurture sequences, and product-led nudges for Billing customers who haven't adopted PSA yet.
3
Fix PSA Conversion, Not Volume
PSA lead volume is healthy (256 leads, 58% of MSP total). The 9.8% close rate is the problem. This is a sales motion and product maturity issue. Marketing's job: deliver qualified leads and better sales enablement materials for competitive PSA objection handling.
4
Reactivate LinkedIn for Billing Campaigns
LinkedIn drove $30K pipeline (All Products) but only $2.5K for PSA. Facebook isn't converting IT MSPs effectively. 2026 bet: reactivate LinkedIn Billing campaigns to target higher-value IT MSP buyers — now with a stronger product story than 2025.
